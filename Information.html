Type of Hikes:
Short Hikes:

Example: Putah creek riparian reserve trail
Short hikes require less planning and are a great place to start for those that do not hike often or are not sure where to start. A short hike could be between 1 and 5 miles round trip.
What to bring: 1: 1 liter or more per and hour of hiking is recomended, 2: Good pair of shoes, 3: Sunscreen



Day Hikes:

Example: Mt. Tallac trail
Day hikes require some ahead of planing. A group leader should plan the hike in advance. These hikes can be 5-15 miles.
What to bring: 1: 1 liter or more per and hour of hiking is recomended, 2: A Pair of hiking shoes, 3: Sunscreen, 4: Map, 5: Meals and snack prepping.



For the more adventurous:
Example: Pacific Crest Trail If you plan to 15 miles are more you should consider camping, or backpacking as these hikes take considerable time and energery. Many of these hikes require permits and or wilderness training.



Local Trail vs State Park vs National Park Local Trails: These are local community trails and may be manged by a number of private or public organizations. These can be free but may have parking and or other fee' State Park: These are aras that the State manages for public use. Genrally these trail heads require fee's or passes to use. National Parks: The more famous sites are manged by the Department of interior. Thise aras require fee's and or passes as well.
